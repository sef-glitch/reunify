# Product Context

## Vision
Reunify is a case management application for families across all U.S. states navigating CPS (Child Protective Services) and family court reunification cases. It empowers parents to organize their cases, track court-ordered service requirements, store proof documents, and generate court-ready packets — all in one place.

**Tagline:** "Organize your case. Reunify your family."

**Scope:** Baseline workflow + 50-state resource directory; no automated state-law rules; AI is informational, not legal advice.

## Target Users
- **Primary:** Parents working toward family reunification in CPS/family court cases across the U.S.
- **Secondary:** Attorneys and caseworkers who receive exported court packets

## Value Proposition
- Centralized tracking of case requirements, deadlines, and hearings
- Weekly action plan aligned with court-ordered service plans
- Secure document vault for certificates, receipts, drug screens, and court documents
- One-click court packet PDF generation with customizable sections
- AI chat assistant for supportive guidance (not legal advice)
- Resource directory for all 50 states (state/category filtered)

## Key Features
1. **Case Management** — Create and track cases with status, county, hearing dates, notes
2. **Task Tracking** — Auto-generated baseline tasks on case creation, priority and due date management
3. **Weekly Action Plan** — Categorized checklist (Court prep, Services, Visits, Stability) with proof upload links
4. **Document Vault** — Tagged uploads (court docs, services, drug screens, housing, employment, visitation)
5. **Court Packet Export** — Configurable PDF with cover page, TOC, summary, checklist, document index
6. **AI Chat Assistant** — GPT-4 powered guidance with legal disclaimers
7. **Resource Directory** — Searchable state/category filtered support resources
8. **Audit Trail** — All mutations logged for compliance

## Important Disclaimer
This platform is for educational and organizational purposes only. It does not provide legal advice. Users should consult an attorney for legal guidance.
